## The Practical

> Immediately "scale" Agile by applying a framework.

Do some googling around and pick something like SAFE, Nexus, or some other "agile for enterprise" solution. Forget about descaling, figuring out how your organization works today, and analyzing how it can work better. And by all means necessary - do _not_ listen to the teams themselves.

> Don't invest in management.

In a culture where management makes decisions, the concept of servant leadership has no place. You cannot risk the political fallout it would mean telling managers they should listen to the people who do the work on how to best do the work.

> Keep your culture the same.

- People content with the way things are.
- What works best for the organization.

> Re-brand personnel titles to be "more Agile".

- Project Managers change to Product Owners or Scrum Masters.

> Transform the entire organization at once.

- Don't start small.
- Champions

> Treat your transformation like a project.

- Time limit.
- Agile coaches/consultants that leave at the end.
- Once its done, its done.

> Profess that Agile is different for everybody.

- The path you tread must be your own journey, making your own mistakes. You cannot take any advice on pitfalls from existing practitioners or literature.

> Wield Agile as a tool to champion whatever you want.

- Because agile is different for everybody, you can weaponize it to destroy any movement that you don't agree with and champion any movement you do.
- Buzzword soup to confuse people.

> Denounce Agile practitioners.

- Stifle discussions that are principled and attempt to change the way teams think and work by accusing them of being too philosophical.

> Keep the power dynamics at the top.

- Servant leadership.
- Decisions still made at top.
- Little to no empowerment of teams to have control.
